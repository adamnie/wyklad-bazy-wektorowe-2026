Course content
1. Lecture
Introduction to the language. Basic constructs and loops. Numeric types. Functions.
Strings, lists, tuples, sets, and dictionaries. Mutable and immutable types. Lambda expressions. Typing.
Advanced topics related to functions. Generators and iterators. Functional programming. Advanced use of lists and dictionaries.
Basics of object orientation. Access to class members, special methods, decorators.
Exceptions. Context manager. Inheritance.
Modules and packages. Boolean data types and evaluation of logical expressions. Good practices in Python.
Regular expressions. Threads and processes. Basics of Django.

Learning outcomes: W1, W2, W3
Class format: Lecture

2. Laboratory classes

As part of the laboratory classes, students individually or in teams carry out a project task, regularly presenting the progress of their work and discussing selected implementation elements. In addition, they become familiar with popular libraries.

Learning outcomes: W1, W2, W3, U1, U2, U3, U4, K1, K2
Class format: Laboratory classes

Additional description

Classes are conducted using innovative teaching methods developed in project POWR.03.04.00-00-D002/16, implemented in 2017–2019 at the Faculty of Computer Science, Electronics and Telecommunications as part of the Operational Programme Knowledge Education Development 2014–2020.

Lectures for this course will be conducted remotely using the Webex/Teams platform.

All other classes will take place in classrooms. This also applies to credits and exams held during examination sessions.

Conditions and method of passing individual class forms, including resit rules, as well as conditions for admission to the exam

The laboratory grade consists of the project grade obtained during the laboratory classes with weight 2 and the theoretical (written) test grade with weight 1; both grades must be positive. The student has the right to one retake of a failing grade (for each component separately), and the final grade is the weighted average of all grades obtained, with the weights as above.

Method of calculating the final grade

The final grade is equal to the laboratory grade. The instructor may modify this grade by up to 0.5 grade points due to the student’s additional activity related to the course that was not included in the laboratory grade.

Method and procedure for making up deficiencies caused by absence from classes

Whenever possible, in justified cases, a student may attend classes with another group.

Initial and additional requirements

Knowledge of basic object-oriented programming concepts.

Rules for participation in individual classes, including whether attendance is compulsory

Lecture: Students attend the lecture, take part in discussion, take notes, and clarify doubts by asking questions. Audio-video recording of the lecture requires the instructor’s consent.
Laboratory classes: Students perform practical work aimed at achieving the competencies assumed in the syllabus. Assessment covers both the method of project implementation and the final result. Attendance at laboratory classes is compulsory.
