Conditions and method of passing individual forms of classes, including rules for resits and conditions for admission to the exam

A condition for passing is obtaining a positive grade in all tests conducted during the semester. If a student receives a failing grade, they have the right to retake it under the following rules:

Retaking a failing grade obtained on the first date results in the failing grade being crossed out and replaced with the passing grade, which will count toward the average.
Retaking a grade obtained on the second date results in the arithmetic mean of the two grades being entered in the grade sheet.
The average partial grade, in the event of passing the test, may not be lower than 3.0.
An unexcused absence from a test results in the loss of that attempt.
Each student has the right to two resit tests.
Tests may take place at intervals of no less than 2 days.
A student has the right to see their assessed work and ask for an explanation of the grading method and the correct answers; however, they are not allowed to photograph or otherwise copy the test materials.

The student is required to have the language course textbook and complete homework assignments. Lack of the required materials (textbook, homework) results in the note “unprepared” being entered in the attendance record. The instructor has the right to refuse to pass a student who was unprepared for more than half of the classes, even if they passed all tests.

The student receives additional grades for class activity, optional homework, presentations, and other activities benefiting their class group.

A condition for admission to the exam is passing the compulsory semesters of the language course.

Method of calculating the final grade

The final grade for the language course is determined as the arithmetic mean of all grades obtained by the student from passing the individual semesters and the foreign language exam.

Method and procedure for making up deficiencies caused by the student’s absence from classes

The student should attend all classes of their language group. In the event of an absence caused by illness, the student should provide a medical certificate no later than 14 days after the absence justified by the certificate. In the event of an unexcused absence, the student should contact the instructor, who will individually determine the method of making up the absence for each student.

The absence must be made up within 14 days from the date of the absence. Two unexcused absences from classes do not require being made up. If the number of unexcused absences exceeds 7, the instructor should refuse to pass the semester, even if some of them had previously been made up.

Initial and additional requirements

Knowledge of English at at least A-2 level.

Rules for participation in individual classes, including whether attendance is compulsory

Language classes: Students attending the classes are required to prepare as indicated each time by the instructor (e.g. in the form of task sets). The assessment of the student’s work may be based on oral or written statements in the form of a test, which, according to the AGH study regulations, translates into the final grade for this form of classes.
