Course content ensuring the achievement of the learning outcomes

The module discusses the structure and operation of basic digital circuits. It allows students to become familiar with methods of designing combinational and sequential circuits and with their practical implementation in FPGA systems.

Course content
1.

Completion of a set of laboratory exercises prepared by the instructor at a workstation equipped with the Multisim simulation environment, and preparation of a detailed written report.

Practical design of digital circuits and their implementation at a workstation equipped with a kit containing a programmable FPGA device.

Learning outcomes: U1, U2, U3
Type of classes: Laboratory classes

2.

The aim of the lecture is to familiarize students with the basics of digital circuit design and the techniques used to design them. Students learn the essence of digital combinational circuits and sequential circuits, as well as the difference between them, and they also learn about the methods and possibilities of practical implementation of designed digital circuits using programmable FPGA logic devices.

Lecture programme:

Introduction to basic definitions, concepts, and issues related to the fundamentals of electronics.
Structure and operating principles of basic logic gates: NOT, AND, OR, NOR, NAND, etc.
Application of NAND and NOR logic gates in the implementation of arbitrary logic functions.
Discussion of logic function minimization methods using laws of logic and Karnaugh maps.
Digital asynchronous and synchronous circuits.
Structure and operating principles of bistable flip-flops (including RS, JK, T, D), monostable and astable circuits.
Types, structure, and operating principles of counters, encoders, decoders, multiplexers, and demultiplexers.
Basics of the operation of multiplier circuits.
Mealy and Moore machines.
Structure of programmable digital devices such as SPLD, CPLD, and FPGA, as well as methods and techniques for configuring them.

The issues presented above are intended to be covered within 28 lecture hours.

Learning outcomes: W1, W2, W3, K1
Type of classes: Lecture

Lectures for this course will be conducted in person or remotely using the Webex/Teams platform.

Other classes (laboratory exercises) will take place in person in designated rooms.

Conditions and method of passing individual forms of classes, including rules for retakes, as well as conditions for admission to the exam

The grade for laboratory classes is awarded by the instructor on the basis of the individual tasks completed by the student and on the basis of the knowledge acquired by the student during lectures and while performing laboratory exercises. This knowledge is verified during a test or through oral responses while the student presents completed laboratory exercises. Detailed information on the course assessment method is provided by the instructor during the first class. Due to the laboratory nature of the course, there is no retake date for passing laboratory classes. The student must pass the course during the regular class period.

Method of calculating the final grade

The final grade is the grade obtained from the laboratory classes.

Method and procedure for making up absences

In the event of a justified and excused absence from a given class, the student may, only with the instructor’s consent, make up the resulting backlog with another laboratory group (if this is physically possible) at a date indicated by the instructor.

Preliminary and additional requirements

Required knowledge in the fundamentals of electrical engineering, electronics, and logic.

Rules for participation in individual classes, including whether attendance is mandatory

Lecture: Students participate in classes, learning successive teaching content in accordance with the course syllabus. Students should ask questions on an ongoing basis and clarify doubts related to the presented material. Audio-visual recording of the lecture REQUIRES the explicit consent of the instructor.

Laboratory classes: Students perform laboratory exercises in accordance with materials provided by the instructor. The student is required to prepare for the exercise being performed, which may be verified orally or in writing. Credit for the classes is granted on the basis of the presented solution to the assigned problem and the submitted detailed report on the course of the exercise. The student must demonstrate full knowledge of the completed laboratory exercise. Passing the entire module is possible only after passing all laboratory classes.
