Conditions and method of passing individual forms of classes, including resit rules, as well as conditions for admission to the exam

The condition for obtaining a pass is receiving a positive grade in all midterm tests conducted during the semester. In the event of receiving a failing grade, the student has the right to retake the assessment according to the following rules:

Retaking a failed grade obtained on the first date results in the failing grade being crossed out and replaced with a passing grade, which will count toward the average.
Retaking a grade obtained on the second date results in the average of the two grades being entered in the record sheet.
The average partial grade, if the test is passed, may not be lower than 3.0.
An unexcused absence from a test results in forfeiture of that test date.
Each student has the right to two resit tests.
Tests may take place at intervals of no less than 2 days.
The student has the right to see their graded work and ask for an explanation of the grading method and the correct answers, but may not photograph or otherwise copy the test materials.

The student is required to have a language learning textbook and complete homework assignments. Lack of the required materials (textbook, homework) results in the notation “unprepared” being entered in the attendance register. The instructor has the right to refuse a pass to a student who was unprepared for more than half of the classes, even if they passed all the tests.

The student receives additional grades for class participation, optional homework, presentations, and other activities for their class group.

A condition for admission to the exam is passing the compulsory semesters of the language course.

Method of calculating the final grade

The final grade for the language course is determined as the arithmetic mean of all grades obtained by the student from passing the individual semesters and the foreign language exam.

Method and procedure for making up deficiencies resulting from the student’s absence from classes

The student should attend all classes of their language group. In the case of absence due to illness, the student should provide a medical certificate no later than 14 days after the absence justified by the certificate. In the case of an unexcused absence, the student should contact the instructor, who will individually determine the method of making up the absence. The absence should be made up within 14 days from the date of absence.

An unexcused absence from two classes does not require make-up work. If the number of unexcused absences exceeds 7, the instructor should refuse to pass the semester, even if some of them were previously made up.
