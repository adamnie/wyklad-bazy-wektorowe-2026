Conditions and method of passing individual class forms, including retake rules, as well as conditions for admission to the exam

A condition for obtaining a pass is receiving a positive grade in all tests conducted during the semester. If a student receives a failing grade, they have the right to retake it according to the following rules:

Retaking a failing grade received at the first sitting results in the failing grade being removed and replaced with the passing grade, which will count toward the average.
Retaking a grade received at the second sitting results in the average of the two grades being entered in the record sheet. In the case of passing the test, the average partial grade cannot be lower than 3.0.
An unexcused absence from a test results in the loss of that sitting.
Each student has the right to two retake tests.
Tests may take place no less than 2 days apart.
A student has the right to see their graded work and ask for an explanation of the grading method and the correct answers; however, they may not photograph or otherwise copy the test materials.

The student is required to have a language textbook and complete homework assignments. Lack of required materials (textbook, homework) results in the entry “unprepared” in the attendance record. The teacher has the right to refuse to pass a student who was unprepared for more than half of the classes, even if they passed all tests.

The student receives additional grades for class activity, optional homework, presentations, and other contributions to their class group.

A condition for admission to the exam is passing the required semesters of the language course.

Method of calculating the final grade

The final grade for the language course is determined as the arithmetic mean of all grades obtained by the student from passing the individual semesters and the foreign language exam.

Method and procedure for making up for absences from classes

The student should attend all classes in their language group. In the case of absence due to illness, the student should provide a medical certificate no later than 14 days after the excused absence. In the case of an unexcused absence, the student should report to the teacher, who will individually specify how the absence is to be made up. The absence must be made up within 14 days of the date of absence.

An unexcused absence from two classes does not require making up. If the number of unexcused absences exceeds 7, the teacher should refuse to pass the semester, even if some of them had been made up earlier.

Initial and additional requirements

Knowledge of French at at least A-2 level.

Rules for participation in individual classes, including whether attendance is compulsory

Language classes: Students attending the classes are required to prepare in the scope indicated each time by the teacher (e.g. in the form of task sets). The assessment of the student’s work may be based on oral or written statements in the form of a test, which, in accordance with the AGH study regulations, contributes to the final grade for this form of classes.
