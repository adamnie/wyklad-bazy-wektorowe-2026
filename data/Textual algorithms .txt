Program content
No. 	Program content 	Course's learning outcomes 	Activities
1.

Fundamentals of Natural Language Processing

Concepts and terminology: tokenization, normalization, syntactic and semantic analysis.
Stages of the NLP process: data preparation, text representation, analysis, and interpretation.

Language representation in computer systems

General principles for creating text representation models (e.g., vectorization, linguistic features).
The impact of data quality on the effectiveness of NLP methods.
	W1, W3 	Lectures, Laboratory classes
2.

Approaches to Language Processing

Characteristics of rule-based, statistical, and machine learning methods.
General assumptions for using language models in text analysis.

Text Data Preparation

Preprocessing techniques: segmentation, noise removal, normalization.
The importance of context and multilingualism in data.
	W2, W4, U1, K1 	Lectures, Laboratory classes
3.

Analysis and evaluation of NLP methods

Comparison of various text representation strategies and their applications.
Identification of sources of error and limitations in the NLP process.
	U2 	Lectures, Laboratory classes
4.

Designing NLP Solutions

General architecture of NLP-based systems: stages, components, integration.

Strategies for dealing with language ambiguity and multilingualism.
	W3, U1 	Lectures, Laboratory classes
5.

Practical Applications of NLP (chosen topics)

Information retrieval and knowledge extraction from text.
Sentiment and opinion analysis.
Dialogue systems and chatbots.
Automatic text translation and summarization.
False information detection and content moderation.
Language processing in business applications (e.g., document analysis, customer service).
	W3, U2, K1 	Lectures, Laboratory classes
