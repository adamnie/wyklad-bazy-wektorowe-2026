Course content
1. Fundamentals of natural language processing
Concepts and terminology: tokenization, normalization, syntactic and semantic analysis.
Stages of the NLP process: data preparation, text representation, analysis, and interpretation.

Language representation in computer systems

General principles of creating text representation models (e.g. vectorization, linguistic features).
The impact of data quality on the effectiveness of NLP methods.

Learning outcomes for the course: W1, W3
Teaching formats: Lecture, Laboratory classes

2. Approaches to language processing
Characteristics of rule-based, statistical, and machine-learning-based methods.
General assumptions behind the use of language models in text analysis.

Preparation of text data

Preprocessing techniques: segmentation, noise removal, normalization.
The importance of context and multilinguality in data.

Learning outcomes for the course: W2, W4, U1, K1
Teaching formats: Lecture, Laboratory classes

3. Analysis and evaluation of NLP methods
Comparison of different text representation strategies and their applications.
Identification of sources of errors and limitations in the NLP process.

Learning outcomes for the course: U2
Teaching formats: Lecture, Laboratory classes

4. Designing NLP solutions
General architecture of systems using NLP: stages, components, integration.
Strategies for handling language ambiguity and multilinguality.

Learning outcomes for the course: W3, U1
Teaching formats: Lecture, Laboratory classes

5. NLP applications in practice (selected topics)
Information retrieval and knowledge extraction from text.
Opinion and sentiment analysis.
Dialogue systems and chatbots.
Automatic translation and text summarization.
Detection of false information and content moderation.
Language processing in business applications (e.g. document analysis, customer service).

Learning outcomes for the course: W3, U2, K1
Teaching formats: Lecture, Laboratory classes

Rules for participation in individual classes, including whether student attendance is mandatory

Lecture: Students participate in classes, learning successive course content in accordance with the course syllabus. Students should regularly ask questions and clarify doubts. Audio-visual recording of the lecture requires the instructor’s consent.

Laboratory classes: Students complete laboratory exercises in accordance with materials provided by the instructor. The student is required to prepare for the exercise being performed, which may be verified by an oral or written test. Passing the class is based on presenting a solution to the assigned problem. Passing the module is possible only after passing all laboratory classes.
